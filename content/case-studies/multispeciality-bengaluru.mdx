## The problem

The hospital had 38 specialties, 240 consultants, and a website that ranked for none of them. The marketing team — three people inside a 1,200-bed institution — was producing what the calendar demanded: World Diabetes Day posts, doctor-of-the-month features, the occasional press release. None of it tied back to a patient walking through OPD.

Their Google Search Console showed something more interesting: 14,000 monthly impressions for high-intent symptom queries (*&ldquo;chest pain causes,&rdquo;* *&ldquo;knee replacement cost Bengaluru&rdquo;*) and a 0.8% click-through rate. The traffic was on the table; it just wasn&rsquo;t coming through their door.

## The 90-day reset

We didn&rsquo;t add new content for the first three months. We restructured what existed.

1. **Audit and triage.** Every page on the site was sorted by patient-journey stage. 40% mapped to Awareness; 4% to Consideration; the Decision stage was empty. The asymmetry explained the funnel.
2. **Rewrite for intent.** The 60 highest-impression-but-low-CTR pages were rewritten in the patient&rsquo;s vocabulary, with the consultant who treats the condition co-authoring. Same URL; different page. Within 6 weeks, average CTR lifted from 0.8% to 4.1%.
3. **Build the Decision layer.** 38 condition-specific landing pages with click-to-call, WhatsApp, and pre-consult prep. Each one tied to a specific specialty and a specific consultant — no homepage, no general &ldquo;contact us.&rdquo;

## What shipped over 18 months

- 142 medically-reviewed long-form articles.
- 38 condition landing pages tied to specialty teams.
- A doctor-led video series on the 12 highest-volume conditions, embedded in the relevant SEO pages.
- A reworked CRM integration: every form submit, call, and WhatsApp ping is now attributed to its source page.

## Outcomes

- **+312% organic enquiry growth** over 18 months.
- **2.4× improvement** in cost per booked OPD on paid search, driven by Quality Score lift on the new landing pages.
- **84% of high-intent searches** now resolve in a session (compared with 23% at start).
- **38 specialties** with at least one ranked condition page, up from 4.

## The compounding bit

The most interesting metric isn&rsquo;t in the deck. The pages we wrote in months 4 and 5 are still our top performers in month 18. That&rsquo;s the whole argument for editorial-grade SEO over campaign-style marketing — once a piece is doing its job, it keeps doing it for years.

Marketing the campaign that did this work would have been louder. Marketing the system that produces it is quieter, and it&rsquo;s why we&rsquo;re still working with this hospital eighteen months later.
