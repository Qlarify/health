Accountability

We report on qualified enquiries, not vanity metrics

Every specialty engagement is governed by a single accountability metric: Cost Per Qualified Lead (CPQL). A qualified lead is a contactable patient enquiry about a real procedure — not a form submission from a competitor's intern, not a 2 AM research click, not a WhatsApp wrong number. We define qualified explicitly at the start of each engagement and hold ourselves to it. If our CPQL runs above the agreed range for two consecutive months, we extend the engagement at no cost until it's corrected. That is the only performance guarantee we offer — and we keep it.

Common questions

Specialty marketing FAQs

Does Qlarify Health work with all hospital specialties?
We have deep playbooks for five high-value specialties: cardiology, IVF & fertility, maternity & paediatrics, oncology, and ophthalmology. We also take on multi-specialty briefs that span several departments simultaneously. If your specialty isn't listed above, speak to us — we've likely worked in adjacent areas.
What is CPQL and why does it matter more than CPL?
CPQL stands for Cost Per Qualified Lead — the marketing spend required to generate one verified, contactable patient enquiry about a real procedure. Unlike standard CPL (cost per lead), CPQL filters out spam, competitor research, wrong-department enquiries, and incomplete form submissions. Across our specialty clients, CPQL runs ₹350–₹1,100 depending on the procedure and city. It's the only metric that maps directly to OPD revenue.
How long before a hospital sees results from specialty marketing?
Paid channels (Google Ads, Meta) typically yield measurable enquiries within weeks 2–4. SEO shows meaningful organic growth in months 3–6 and compounds from there. Specialty video libraries start generating inbound queries within 60 days of publishing. Most hospitals see 3–6x ROI within the first year.
Why does specialty-specific marketing outperform generic hospital marketing?
Each specialty has a distinct patient decision journey, search vocabulary, caregiver dynamic, and trust threshold. Generic hospital landing pages convert at 0.8–1.5%. Specialty-specific pages — with procedure-level keywords, appropriate content depth, and correct schema markup — convert at 3–6%. The difference compounds over hundreds of enquiries per month.
Does Qlarify comply with MCI/NMC advertising guidelines?
Yes. Every content piece is reviewed against NMC (formerly MCI) advertising norms — no before/after claims, no guaranteed outcomes, no comparative superiority claims. Our DPDP-compliant lead capture forms use explicit opt-in consent. We also follow the ASCI Healthcare Advertising Code for social media content. Compliance review is included in every engagement; it is not an add-on.
Can a single-location hospital work with Qlarify, or is this only for large chains?
We work with both. Single-location hospitals in tier-1 and tier-2 cities often see the fastest ROI — hyperlocal SEO and Google Business Profile optimisation alone can drive 25–40% OPD growth within 6 months. Large chains benefit most from our multi-specialty, multi-city content architecture and CPQL reporting infrastructure.
