The cardiac patient's digital reality

Your patient is researching online before they ever call your hospital.

A chest pain at 2am, an abnormal TMT report, a cardiologist's referral to a cardiac surgeon. At every stage of this journey the patient or caregiver is on a phone searching, comparing, deciding. Cardiac programmes that show up with the right content at each of these moments earn the enquiry before the call is made.

🔍

Stage 1 — Symptom search

"Chest pain at night Bangalore." "Heart palpitations what does it mean." "ECG abnormal what next." These searches happen before the appointment is booked. Centres with calm, clinician-led content at this stage build early trust.

🏥

Stage 2 — Hospital research

"Best cardiologist Bangalore." "Top cardiac surgery hospital India." "Narayana vs Manipal heart surgery." The patient is now comparing centres. Centres without procedure-specific content, surgeon profiles, and visible NABH accreditation lose at this stage.

⚖️

Stage 3 — Procedure decision

"Angioplasty vs bypass surgery which is better." "CABG success rate India." "TAVR procedure cost Bangalore." The patient is deciding between procedures. Decision-aid content from your cardiac surgeons and cardiologists can determine which centre earns the admission.

Why most cardiac programmes underperform digitally

Generic hospital marketing doesn't grow a cardiac programme.

Frequently asked questions · Cardiology

What CMOs and cardiac programme heads ask before engaging us.

How does NABH accreditation affect our cardiology marketing?
NABH accreditation is a powerful trust signal for high-stakes cardiac decisions but most hospitals bury it in a footer logo. We integrate NABH signalling directly into procedure-level content — landing pages for CABG, TAVR, and angioplasty — so patients encounter your accreditation at the moment they are comparing centres. We also communicate what NABH means in plain language: infection control standards, surgical protocols, ICU staffing ratios. For patients choosing between Narayana, Manipal, and your centre, a clearly communicated NABH or JCI status can shift the decision in your favour.
What is the typical CPQL for cardiology marketing in India?
Our cardiology engagements target a Cost Per Qualified Lead (CPQL) of ₹450–₹850 per enquiry, depending on the sub-specialty and geography. Preventive cardiology and general cardiac OPD enquiries typically come in at the lower end of this range. High-acuity procedures like TAVR, CABG, and complex electrophysiology sit toward the upper end because the audience is smaller and search volumes are lower. We track CPQL per sub-specialty — not blended across all cardiology — so your team can see exactly what each procedure costs to generate enquiries for.
How do you handle the emergency-window (chest pain at 2am) search intent?
Cardiac events peak in the early morning hours, and families search before they call an ambulance. We build calm, clear emergency-guidance content — SEO-optimised landing pages and short videos — targeting searches like "chest pain tonight Bangalore" and "heart attack symptoms what to do". This content is designed to capture intent before a call is made, provide immediate guidance, and surface your emergency line and nearest facility. It is among the highest-conversion content we produce for cardiac programmes because the patient's urgency is matched to your availability.
Can you help our cardiac centre compete with Narayana and Manipal?
Yes. Narayana and Manipal set the benchmark in the public mind for cardiac care quality, which means independent centres and smaller hospital groups need systematic digital signalling to be considered alongside them. We map your programme's genuine differentiators — cath-lab technology, MDT structure, surgeon case volumes, NABH/JCI status, wait times, geography — and communicate them through procedure-specific content, SEO, and paid media. We do not manufacture differentiation that does not exist, but most cardiac programmes have significantly more to say than their current digital presence shows.
How do you manage referral transparency between GP, cardiologist, and cardiac surgery?
Referral leakage between GP, medical cardiologist, and cardiac surgeon is one of the least-discussed sources of lost cardiac volume. We produce content at each referral stage — GP-facing resources on when to refer to cardiology, cardiologist-facing content on cardiac surgery indications, and patient-facing decision-aid material on the angioplasty vs CABG choice. We also build referring-physician WhatsApp programmes and LinkedIn outreach to keep your cardiac team visible to the GP and physician community. UTM tagging allows us to track enquiry source back to each referral channel so you can quantify the referral path ROI.
Do you cover both medical cardiology and cardiac surgery in one engagement?
Yes. A single Qlarify engagement covers both the medical cardiology path (arrhythmia, hypertension, coronary artery disease, heart failure, electrophysiology, preventive cardiology) and the cardiac surgery path (CABG, valve replacement, TAVR, pacemaker, ICD). Content, SEO, and paid media are built to address the full patient journey from initial symptom search through OPD and, where indicated, IPD admission. Sub-specialty CPQL is tracked separately so you can see the return on each path independently and allocate budget accordingly.
